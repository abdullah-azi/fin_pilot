Design decisions

Month summary strip

Total in, net, and total out as a compact 3-column strip — the user instantly sees the month's health before scrolling through individual transactions.

Grouped by date, not category

Chronological grouping matches how people think about their spending — "what did I buy yesterday?" not "show me all transport." Category filtering handles the other use case.

Filter chips + search together

Chips handle broad filters (All / Expenses / Income / Month). Search handles specific lookups. Both work simultaneously — filter to Expenses, then search "groceries."

Red = money out

Consistent with the home screen. Every debit is red, every credit is green — no ambiguity even when scanning quickly.

Category total on filter

When filtered to a category, a summary card appears below showing total spent and % of budget — with an "Ask AI" shortcut to jump into a conversation about it.

Time shown, not just date

Each transaction shows the time (2:14 PM) — useful for spotting patterns like late-night spending, and helps users match receipts to entries.
