Design decisions

Empty state isn't empty

Four suggested questions based on the most common things users ask. Reduces the blank page anxiety of "what do I even ask?"

Violet = AI identity

AI avatar, bubbles, and typing indicator are all violet — the same signal used across the app. Users always know which messages came from the AI.

Snapshot card, not just text

The AI responds with a structured data card first, then a plain-language verdict below. Data builds trust; language makes it feel personal.

Amber = caution verdict

"Possible but tight" uses amber — not red (which would be wrong) and not green. The color matches the nuance of the answer.

AI offers to take action

"Want me to log a recurring Rs 5,800 expense?" — the AI doesn't just advise, it offers to act. This is the biggest differentiator from a static budgeting app.

Conversation builds context

The follow-up "what if installments?" shows the AI carries context from the previous message — it doesn't re-explain the snapshot, it builds on it.
