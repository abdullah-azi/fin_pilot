Design decisions

3-color status system

On track = green, Behind = amber, At risk = red. Same semantic rules as the rest of the app. The border tint on each card reinforces the status without needing to read the badge.

AI recommendation strip first

Before the user reads individual goals, the AI tells them the one number that matters — how much they can save comfortably this month and whether they're on track overall.

Monthly pace on every card

"Save Rs 8,200/mo to hit target" is more actionable than a progress bar alone. When it turns red ("Rs 23,000/mo — too aggressive") the user immediately knows to extend the deadline.

AI projection in the add form

As soon as the user enters a target amount and date, the AI card calculates the monthly needed and what % of income that is. Green = easy, amber = stretch, red = unrealistic.

Green CTA for goals

The "Create goal" button is green — the only screen where the primary action isn't violet. Green here means saving/growth, keeping the semantic color rules consistent.

Icon picker for personality

Letting users pick an icon makes goals feel personal and motivating — a plane for a trip, a shield for emergency fund. Small touch, big impact on engagement.
