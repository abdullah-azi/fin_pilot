Design decisions

Two scrollable halves

Both screens are the same page — the user scrolls from the donut + category bars straight into behavior score and AI insights. Split here just to show both halves clearly.

Donut over pie

The hollow center shows the total spent — two pieces of info in one visual. Tapping a donut segment should highlight that category row below it.

Month-on-month delta

Every category row shows ↑↓→ vs last month. That single comparison is more actionable than the absolute number alone.

Trend bar — current month highlighted

The current month bar is violet; past months are gray; next month is a dashed outline (projected). The dot above May marks it as the active period.

Behavior score — not a judgment

"Moderate spender" is framed as a pattern, not a grade. The spec explicitly says behavioral feedback should be informative, not financial judgment — the tone here reflects that.

3-color insight cards

Red = act now, amber = watch this, green = doing well. Same semantic color rules as the rest of the app — no new mental model to learn.

Ask AI shortcut at the bottom

After reading their insights a user's natural next question is "so what do I do?" — the violet AI card bridges directly into the assistant without navigating away.
